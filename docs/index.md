# Surfacing Mining

The primary value of this project is the framework.

The framework surfaces the distribution of value, cost, and exposure across regions and time.

The included data has not been vetted and is provided solely to illustrate the framework structure.
Authoritative values should be derived from engineering studies, regulatory filings, and validated sources.

## Typical Discovery Workflow

This project reflects how information is typically surfaced and assembled:

1. Identify a project or region of interest
2. Locate regulatory actions and permits
3. Review project disclosures and technical reports
4. Determine site location and surrounding context
5. Map watershed and divide relationships (where applicable)
6. Identify value flows (local vs external)
7. Identify exposures and their persistence over time
8. Link claims to supporting sources

The explorer is designed to make this structure explicit and inspectable.

## Additional Resources

### Regulatory and Permitting

- [Congressional Review Act (CRA)](https://crsreports.congress.gov/product/pdf/IF/IF10023)
  Mechanism used by Congress to review and potentially overturn federal regulations.

- [Federal Register](https://www.federalregister.gov/)
  Official source for proposed and final federal rules, notices, and regulatory actions.

- [U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA)](https://www.epa.gov/)
  Federal environmental regulation, permitting frameworks, and impact assessments.

- [Minnesota Department of Natural Resources (DNR)](https://www.dnr.state.mn.us/)
  State-level permitting, environmental review, and resource management.

- [Minnesota Pollution Control Agency (MPCA)](https://www.pca.state.mn.us/)
  Water, air, and environmental permitting and monitoring.

### Project and Company Information

- Company technical reports (e.g., feasibility studies, NI 43-101 reports)
- Investor presentations and filings
- Environmental Impact Statements (EIS) and Environmental Assessment Worksheets (EAW)

### Geospatial and Site Context

- [USGS National Map](https://apps.nationalmap.gov/)
  Topography, hydrology, and geospatial base layers.

- [Minnesota Geospatial Commons](https://gisdata.mn.gov/)
  State geospatial datasets including watersheds, land use, and infrastructure.

- [USGS Watershed Boundary Dataset (WBD)](https://www.usgs.gov/national-hydrography/watershed-boundary-dataset)
  Hydrologic unit boundaries for watershed analysis.

- Laurentian Divide datasets (via USGS / Minnesota GIS sources)
  Defines directional flow boundaries between major drainage basins.

### Supporting Context

- County zoning and land use records
- Local government planning documents
- Infrastructure maps (transmission, roads, rail)
- Public hearing records and meeting minutes
