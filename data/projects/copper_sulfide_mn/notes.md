# copper_sulfide_mn: Analysis Notes

## Regional context

Northeast Minnesota hosts the Duluth Complex, a geological formation containing
significant copper, nickel, cobalt, and platinum group metal deposits. Proposed
and previously proposed projects include PolyMet/NorthMet (Hoyt Lakes/Babbitt area)
and Twin Metals Minnesota (Ely area, adjacent to BWCAW).

The region sits astride the **Laurentian Divide**: drainage flows both north
into the Rainy River watershed (Hudson Bay) and south/east into Lake Superior
(St. Lawrence Seaway). This bifurcated hydrology means any discharge or AMD
event can affect two distinct international watersheds.

## Key structural tensions

- **Local employment** vs. **local environmental exposure**: most employment
  benefit accrues to the region, but so do water quality and recreational risks.
- **Short mine life vs. long remediation tail**: 20-30 year operations,
  potentially perpetual water treatment post-closure.
- **External commodity value vs. local cost bearing**: metal revenue flows to
  global markets; reclamation liability remains local/state.
- **Permitting jurisdiction split**: state permitting (MPCA, DNR) vs. federal
  (Army Corps, Forest Service, EPA), with different risk tolerances.

## Data quality notes

Exposures and value flow weights are structural defaults from the
`copper_sulfide_hardrock` profile. They are not calibrated to a specific project.
Site-specific calibration requires:
- Actual ore body tonnage and grade estimates
- Site geochemical characterization (NP/AP ratio, acid base accounting)
- Independent recreation/tourism economic baseline
- Tribal consultation records
